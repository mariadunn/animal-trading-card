Sawfish

"How SAW-some to see you!"

Baby sawfish are born live; the teeth on their rostrums are encased in a sheath to prevent them from injuring their mother.

  • Also Known As: Carpenter Sharks
  • Colour: Light Grey or Brown
  • IUCN Status: At least Endangered
  • Sawfish Celebrity: Yugwurrirrindangwa, creator of the Angurugu river (NT, Australia)

The sawfish is a type of ray found in the Atlantic and Indo-Pacific regions. The rostrum (snout) of the sawfish is used to detect and kill its prey, as well as defending itself against predators. Although fearsome-looking, sawfish are generally docile and pose little threat to humans. Their rostrums are prized in several cultures across the world.
